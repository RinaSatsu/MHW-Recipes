Meowscular Chef's Platter: Seafood Paella


Hot and hearty seafood paella made from freshly-hunt Juratodus

Serves: 8-10

Ingredients:

  • 1 onioin
  • spring onions
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 carrot
  • 2 cups dry white wine
  • 3 cups paella rice
  • 2 cups (400 ml) stock
  • 14 oz or 400 ml tomato paste (canned tomatoes work as well)
  • 4 large prawns
  • 4 mussels, clean + debearded
  • Baby cuttlefish
  • Green onions
  • Green peas

For shrimp stock:

  • 1 lb or 500 g jumbo shrimp
  • >vegetable oil
  • 1 carrot, cut into 3 inch portions
  • 1 celery stalk, cut into 3 inch portions
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1-2 parsley stems
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 Liter water

Steps:

  1. Cut onion in small pieces.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  3. Add onion and spring onion and fry until golden.
  4. Slice bell pepper and carrotinto long strips. Add to the pan and fry for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Deglaze with wine. Add rice and stock then mix todether.
  6. Add tomato paste and mix everything once more.
  7. Preheat the oven to 375 °F (190 °C).Transfer rice into large bowl and put into oven for 30 minutes.
  8. Meanwhile, heat oil in pan. Fry prawns, mussels and baby cuttlefish.
  9. Take out the rice. Add cuttlefish, green onions and peas. Mix together and put in large frying pan (or bowl) for serving.
  10. Put prawns and mussels on top of rice in cross-like pattern and serve.

For shrimp stock:

  1. Remove the shells from the shrimp. Reserve or freeze the shrimp for later use.
  2. Add the oil to a large stock pot and begin heating over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the shrimp shells, cook until the shells brighten in color and become aromatic, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the remaining ingredients and bring the stock to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and allow the stock to simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
  6. Strain and reserve the stock.
