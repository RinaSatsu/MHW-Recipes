Meowscular Chef's Platter: Homemade Sausage

Image source


Hearty homemade Aptonoth sausages


Serves: 8-10

Ingredients:

  • Sausage casings
  • 4 lb or 2 kg sirloin, trimmed
  • 1 lb or 500 g pork fat back
  • Curing salt
  • 28 g kosher salt
  • 5 g freshly ground black pepper
  • Fennel
  • Harissa
  • Aleppo pepper
  • Garlic powder

Steps:

  1. Place grinding tools in the freezer for 1 hour before using.
  2. Prepare the sausage casing by rinsing them thoroughly and then allowing them to sit in warm water for 1-2 hours. Replace the water periodically.
  3. Cut the sirloin and fat back into 1-2” cubes.
  4. Transfer the meats to a rimmed baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Place the tray in the freezer to allow the meat to firm up before grinding, about 20-30 minutes.
  5. Using the largest grinder setting or slide, grind the chilled meat. Make sure for every two pieces of sirloin, you add one piece of fat.
  6. Transfer the ground meat back to the baking sheet and freeze for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Pass the ground meat through the ginder once more using the smallest grinder setting or slide. Make sure to weigh the meat mixture after grinding in order to calculate the amount of curing salt needed.
  8. Once again, transfer the meat to the baking sheet and freeze for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Meanwhile, combine the curing salt, salt, pepper, fennel, harissa, aleppo pepper, and garlic powder in a small bowl. Whisk to combine.
  10. Add the spice mixture to the meat and mix by hand until the mixture becomes sticky.
  11. Fill the sausage casings with the mixture using the extruder tool.
  12. Transfer the sausages to a large rimmed baking sheet. Refrigerate overnight or up to 24 hours.
  13. Remove the sausages from the fridge for 1-2 hours before smoking to allow all the moisture to evaporate.
  14. Prepare your smoker with your preference of wood chips.
  15. Before smoking, prik the sausages with a small needle or skewer to avoid bursting.
  16. Smoke the sausages at 200 °F (95 °C) for 2-3 hours or until the internal temperature reaches 160 °F (70 °C).
  17. Allow the sausage to cool before grilling.
  18. Grill for 2-3 minutes on each side.
